This continues the thought from the verse before it, but pushes it into somewhere a little more actionable. Sada tad-bhava-bhavitah — always dwelling in that state. Not once. Not occasionally. Constantly, as a practiced habit, until it becomes the groove your mind falls into without effort. I think about this less in terms of death and more in terms of the ordinary, boring repetition of an average Tuesday. What do you dwell on while you're doing the dishes, or driving somewhere familiar, or lying awake at 2am? That unglamorous, repetitive dwelling is quietly building the shape of your mind, verse by verse, thought by thought, long before any dramatic moment tests it. It's a little sobering, honestly, but also freeing. It means you're not stuck with whatever pattern you've had until now. The groove can be redug, slowly, the same way it was dug in the first place — through repetition, not through one big decision made once and never revisited. So maybe the useful question isn't 'what do I believe,' but 'what do I keep returning to, without noticing.' That quieter habit is the one actually shaping you. It's worth watching gently, and redirecting, a little at a time.
