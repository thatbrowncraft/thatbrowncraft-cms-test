Bahunam janmanam — after many lifetimes. Not one good week of clarity. Not a single breakthrough retreat. Many lifetimes. He's stretching the timeline out to something almost unimaginably long, and saying that's still the ordinary pace at which this kind of understanding tends to arrive. I think that's meant to be comforting, even though it sounds at first like it might be discouraging. Because if the honest timeline is that long, then whatever pace you're moving at — however slow it feels, however many times you've circled back to the same lesson — you're not behind some invisible schedule. There was never a fast lane you missed. There's no frustration in his voice here. No 'why does it take you all so long.' Just a plain, almost tender acknowledgment that this kind of seeing — vasudevah sarvam iti, all of this is divine — is rare precisely because it asks so much unlearning, so much accumulated living, before it can finally settle in. So if you feel like you've been circling the same realization for years without quite landing on it, that's not evidence you're doing something wrong. That might just be exactly how long it takes. He seems to already know that, and he doesn't seem impatient about it at all.
