There's something almost relieving about how honest this verse is. He's not promising that everyone who tries will get there quickly, or that spiritual understanding is easy to reach if you just apply yourself. He's saying plainly — it's rare. Most people don't even attempt it. Of those who do, most don't get all the way. It's a long road, and that's simply true. I think we sometimes carry a quiet guilt about not having 'figured it out' yet, whatever our version of that is. Watching other people seem more settled, more sure, more arrived. This verse gently removes the pressure of that comparison. If it's rare even among those who are seriously trying, then not being there yet was never evidence that something is wrong with you. I find it strangely comforting to be told the truth here instead of an encouraging exaggeration. Not 'anyone can get there overnight if they just believe.' Just — this is hard, it takes most people a long time, and some never fully arrive, and that's simply the nature of the path, not a personal failing. So if you're still striving, still not sure you understand any of it — you're not behind. You're just on the road that was always going to be long.
