There's something so intimate about aham atma, sarva-bhutashaya-sthitah — I am the Self, seated in the heart of every being. Not observing from a distance. Not visiting occasionally. Seated. Settled. Already there, in the quietest room of you, before you even knew to look. I think we often search for the sacred somewhere far away — a special place, a rare moment, a mountaintop kind of experience. This verse gently redirects that search inward, and not just for exceptional people either. Sarva-bhutashaya-sthitah. Every being. Not just the saints. Not just the ones who've earned it. Everyone, including you, exactly as you are right now, mid-argument or mid-grief or mid-ordinary-Tuesday. And then the second half — the beginning, the middle, and the end. Not just present at the good parts. Present at the start of something, present through the messy middle where nothing feels resolved yet, and present at the ending too, whatever shape that ending takes. So if you're in the middle of something right now, unfinished and unclear, this verse says he's not waiting at the end for you to arrive. He's already there, in the middle with you, seated quietly, the whole way through.
