This is the very first thing Arjuna says once he actually looks at the battlefield. Not a philosophy. Not a question. Just a body falling apart in real time — shaking hands, dry mouth, legs that won't hold. I love that the Gita opens here and not somewhere more composed. It means the whole conversation that follows, all eighteen chapters of it, was spoken to someone who was, in that moment, not okay. Not performing wisdom. Not pretending to be fine. I think we've all had a version of this. Standing in front of something we can't avoid anymore, and the body just tells the truth before we're ready to say it out loud. That shaking isn't weakness. It's just what happens when a person is honest with themselves for the first time in a while. Kanha didn't rush him past this. He let him sit in it, ask his questions, even refuse to fight, before saying a single word of guidance. If you're standing somewhere shaky right now, that's alright. This is where the whole thing begins. Not at the answer. Here, at the trembling.
