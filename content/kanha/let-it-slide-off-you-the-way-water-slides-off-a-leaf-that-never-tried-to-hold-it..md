I love this image more than almost any other in the whole text. A lotus leaf doesn't repel water by trying hard. It's just built in such a way that water simply can't cling to its surface. There's no effort in it, no straining to stay clean. It's just the nature of the leaf. That's such a different picture than the version of resilience we're usually sold — the gritted-teeth kind, where you have to fight to not let things stick to you. This verse is describing something gentler. A kind of living where things pass through you instead of collecting on you, not because you're numb, but because you were never trying to hold onto them in the first place. I think about this on the days when criticism or a bad outcome or someone's harsh words feel like they're soaking into me instead of running off. It helps to remember that clinging isn't actually required. The water doesn't have to sit there. I don't have to let it. It's not about becoming unfeeling. The lotus still touches the water. It just doesn't keep it. That's the whole difference, and it's available to anyone willing to practice it, one drop at a time.
