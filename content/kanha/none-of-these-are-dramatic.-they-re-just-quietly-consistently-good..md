I like how unglamorous this list is. No mention of miracles, or grand achievements, or visible signs of holiness. Just a set of quiet, everyday qualities — steadiness, forgiveness, a lack of malice, a lack of needing to feel superior. The kind of things a person can practice on an ordinary Tuesday, without anyone noticing or applauding. I think that's the actual point being made here. Divine nature, in this verse, isn't reserved for the exceptional or the extraordinary. It's built out of small, repeatable, almost boring virtues — the kind that don't make for dramatic stories, but that quietly shape a person into someone genuinely good to be around. There's something relieving in that. It means you don't need a dramatic spiritual breakthrough to move toward this. You just need to practice forgiving a little more easily, holding your steadiness a little longer, letting go of the need to be seen as important. Small, unglamorous, repeated choices. On a day when nothing about your life feels particularly remarkable, this verse offers a quiet reassurance. The path being described here was never about being remarkable. It was about being kind, steady, and unbothered by your own importance, one ordinary day at a time.
