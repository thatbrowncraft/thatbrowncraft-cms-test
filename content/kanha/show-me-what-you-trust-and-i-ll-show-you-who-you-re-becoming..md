Shraddhamayo 'yam purushah — a person is made of their faith. Not shaped by it, at some distance. Made of it. Whatever you place your trust in, again and again, becomes the material you're built from, quietly, over time, whether you're paying attention to the process or not. I think this is worth sitting with, because faith here doesn't only mean religious belief. It means whatever you actually trust to be true and reliable — a certain way of relating to people, a certain story about your own worth, a certain assumption about how the world tends to work. Whatever that is, this verse says, is quietly constructing who you are. That's a little unsettling if you look closely at where your faith has actually been placed lately. But it's also hopeful, because it means the process is ongoing, not fixed. What you place your trust in tomorrow starts building something new, the same slow way today's faith built what you are right now. So maybe the useful question, from this verse, isn't 'what do I believe intellectually.' It's 'what do I actually trust, in my bones, on an ordinary day.' That quieter answer is the one shaping you, brick by brick, whether you meant it to or not.
