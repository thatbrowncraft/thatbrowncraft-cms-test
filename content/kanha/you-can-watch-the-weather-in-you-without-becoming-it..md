Udasinavad — like a witness, seated to the side, watching rather than being pulled into whatever's happening. I love how physical this image is. Not fighting the gunas, not trying to force calm onto restlessness or clarity onto heaviness. Just sitting, a little apart, watching them move without being dragged along by the current. There's something in guna vartanta iti — simply knowing 'the qualities are at play' — that feels almost scientific in its plainness. Not 'I am failing' when tamas shows up as heaviness, or 'I am finally succeeding' when sattva shows up as clarity. Just, quietly, 'ah, this quality is active right now.' A gentle observation, not a verdict. I think this is one of the more practical teachings in the whole text, because it doesn't ask you to eliminate the gunas, which isn't really possible anyway — they're built into nature itself. It asks for something smaller and more achievable: a seat slightly to the side, watching without getting swept in. On a day when restlessness or heaviness is loud, this verse offers a doable practice. Not fixing the weather. Just sitting where you can watch it pass, and knowing, quietly, that you were never the weather to begin with.
