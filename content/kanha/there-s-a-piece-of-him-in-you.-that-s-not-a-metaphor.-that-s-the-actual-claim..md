Mamaivamsho — a fragment of me, myself. Not something separate from him, created and set apart. An actual piece, sanatana, eternal, the same substance, simply expressed in the specific shape of a living being, drawing a mind and senses around itself the way a magnet gathers what's nearby. I think it's worth sitting with how direct this claim is. It's not saying you're loved by something divine, from a respectful distance. It's saying something of that divine actually constitutes you, at the core, underneath the mind and senses that gathered around it. Not a visitor in you. A fragment of the source itself, wearing your particular life as its current expression. I find this changes something quietly, on the days when I feel especially small, or flawed, or far from anything sacred. This verse doesn't ask you to become worthy of containing something divine. It says you already do, structurally, at the most basic level of what you are, regardless of how the day is going. So the practice, maybe, isn't earning this. It's remembering it. The fragment doesn't need to be installed. It's already there, quietly, underneath everything else you've gathered around yourself since.
