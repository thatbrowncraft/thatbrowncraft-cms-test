After a whole chapter about the constantly shifting gunas — the moods, energies, and states that rise and fall through every person, every day — this closing verse offers something to stand on underneath all of that shifting. Pratishtha — foundation. Not another passing state. The floor beneath all the states. I think this matters because so much of what we feel does genuinely change, sometimes within a single day. Clarity in the morning, restlessness by afternoon, heaviness by evening. If that were the whole story, stability would feel impossible, always one guna away from disappearing again. But this verse says there's something underneath all of it that isn't shifting the same way — sukhasya aikantikasya, joy that isn't dependent on circumstance, unending in a way ordinary happiness, tied to mood and outcome, never quite manages to be. I find it comforting to think of him this way — not as another feeling to chase, competing with all your other feelings for space, but as the ground those feelings are all standing on, unmoved beneath them. On a day when your own ground feels shaky, it might help to remember there's a floor further down than your current mood. He said he's it.
