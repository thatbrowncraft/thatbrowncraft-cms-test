I think of this verse whenever something ordinary suddenly stops me — a particular color in the sky at the end of the day, a piece of music that catches in my chest for no clear reason, the specific, undeserved kindness of a stranger. This verse says those moments aren't accidents, or separate from him, or small distractions from something more serious. They're glimpses. Fragments. Tejo-'msha — just a portion of splendor, showing up briefly in something small. There's something very generous about that framing. It means the sacred isn't only found in temples or scripture or formal devotion. It's found in whatever, today, made you stop and feel something larger than yourself for a moment, even if you couldn't name why. I think this changes how I hold beauty now. It's not decoration on top of life. It's evidence, brief and specific, of something much larger reaching through in a form small enough for a person to actually notice. So the next time something ordinary takes your breath away, without warning, for reasons you can't fully explain — you might not need to look for a deeper meaning elsewhere. This verse says you already found it. That was him, waving, in whatever shape reached you.
