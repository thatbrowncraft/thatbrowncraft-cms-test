I keep coming back to this image — pearls on a thread. Individually, each pearl looks separate, distinct, unconnected to the one beside it. But there's a single thread running through all of them, invisible unless you look closely, holding the whole necklace together so it doesn't scatter across the floor. That's such a gentle way of describing how everything fits together, especially on days when life feels like a pile of disconnected pearls — this worry, that responsibility, this relationship, that grief, none of them seeming to relate to each other, none of them making sense as a whole. This verse is quietly saying there's a thread running through all of it, even the parts that feel chaotic and separate. I don't think this means everything happens for a tidy reason, or that hardship is secretly fine because it's 'part of a plan.' I think it means something simpler and less tidy than that — that you are held together by something continuous, even when your days don't feel continuous at all. On the days when everything in your life feels like separate beads rolling in different directions, it might help to remember: there's still a thread. You just can't always see it from where you're standing.
