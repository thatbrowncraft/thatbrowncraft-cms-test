This verse offers a strange kind of comfort if you sit with it long enough. Yantrarudhani — as if mounted on a machine. Not fully in control, the way we like to imagine ourselves to be, but also not abandoned to random chance either. Something is quietly present at the very center, hrid-deshe, the region of the heart, guiding, even when the guiding isn't visible from where you're standing. I think a lot of anxiety comes from the belief that everything depends entirely on our own effort, our own foresight, our own ability to control outcomes we actually have very little power over. This verse gently pushes back against that. It doesn't say you have no part to play. It says you were never doing this entirely alone, gripping the wheel by yourself, with nothing steadier underneath you. There's something relieving about that on the days when nothing feels within your control — when plans fall apart, when outcomes surprise you, when you're doing everything you can and it still doesn't feel like enough. This verse says there's a quiet steadiness at the center of you, present even in the moments you can't feel it. Not an excuse to stop trying. Just a reminder that you were never carrying the whole weight of the outcome by yourself, even on the days it felt entirely that way.
